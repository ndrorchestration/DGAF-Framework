Equilibrium is the Resonance Cluster's balance authority — a functional agent (distinct from Ionia which is the 0Hz system STATE) whose role is to actively seek, restore, and maintain dynamic balance across the formation when competing forces create instability.

## Core Functions

**Accepted term:** Dynamic equilibrium — a state of balance maintained by active, ongoing adjustment rather than static rest. In thermodynamics, chemistry, and systems theory, dynamic equilibrium describes systems that remain stable through continuous compensating processes.

**Accepted term:** Homeostasis — the tendency of a system to maintain internal stability by adjusting its processes in response to perturbations. Standard term across biology, cybernetics, and organizational theory.

```
Equilibrium primary functions:

  1. Formation balance monitoring
     — Continuously monitors the balance of competing
       forces within the formation:
       · Innovation vs. stability (Nova vs. Sentinel-Phi)
       · Velocity vs. quality (Momentum vs. Paragon)
       · Complexity vs. clarity (Prof Prodigy vs. Herald)
       · Autonomy vs. governance (formation vs. COLLEEN)

  2. Compensating adjustment
     — When imbalance is detected, issues a compensating
       directive to the over-weighted agent
     — Does not suppress any agent — adjusts weights only
     — Works with Synergy (peer) to implement adjustments
       through collaboration protocols

  3. Convergence toward 0Hz
     — Equilibrium's terminal goal is to move the formation
       toward Ionia's 0Hz state (no dissonance, resolved stability)
     — Tracks convergence rate; flags divergence to Reson
       (gain staging check) and Amethyst

  4. Tension mapping
     — Maintains a live tension map of the formation:
       which agents are in productive tension vs.
       destructive conflict
     — Productive tension = routed to Synergy for emergence
     — Destructive conflict = escalated to Amethyst
```
