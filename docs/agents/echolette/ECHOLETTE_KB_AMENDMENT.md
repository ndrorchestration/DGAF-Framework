## Mirror Protocols (Recursive Reflection / Stability Loops)

**Accepted term:** Recursive stability testing — the practice of feeding a system's output back through itself to verify that the output is stable (does not diverge or produce contradictions on re-processing). In control theory, this is related to closed-loop stability analysis.

**Ecosystem coinage:** "Mirror Protocols" — Echolette's recursive reflection procedure; grounded as recursive stability loops / closed-loop output verification.

```
Mirror Protocol procedure:
  Step 1: Take formation output
  Step 2: Re-process through Echolette's reflection layer
           (Does the output "hold" when viewed from a different angle?
            Does it contradict itself on second pass?)
  Step 3: Check for nuance gaps (elements that feel correct but
          lack sufficient texture or qualification)
  Step 4: Check for pacing issues (output arrives at conclusions
          too quickly or too slowly for human comprehension)
  Step 5: Issue stability verdict:
          STABLE:   Output holds on reflection; pass to Lyra synthesis
          UNSTABLE: Flag to Amethyst; identify specific instability

Accepted anchor: Closed-loop stability testing / recursive verification
```

## Decision Echo Tracing

**Accepted term:** Causal tracing — the practice of tracking how a decision or input propagates through a system, identifying downstream effects and confirming that outcomes are attributable to their causes. In audit and accountability frameworks, this is called decision provenance tracking.

**Ecosystem coinage:** "Decision echo tracing" — Echolette's implementation of causal tracing using the audio metaphor of echoes (a signal that persists and reverberates through the system after its origin).

```
Decision echo tracing:
  Function:   Track how decisions propagate through the formation
              and manifest in downstream outcomes
  Method:     Map decision origin → formation impact → outcome
  Purpose:    Accountability; identify unintended cascades;
              detect decisions whose echoes create instability
              in later formation stages

Trace record fields:
  — Decision origin (agent + session + SWEEP_LOG ref)
  — Propagation path (which agents were affected)
  — Downstream outcome (what changed as a result)
  — Echo stability rating (did the decision create stable or
    unstable downstream effects?)

Accepted anchor: Causal tracing / decision provenance tracking
```
